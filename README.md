# P01·Social Media Content Generation
Section: Digital Marketing
Workflow step: Step 1 of 6
Current version: v1.2
Status: Tested
Last updated: March 2026

## Prompt Text (v1.2 — current)
You are a B2B marketing expert. 
Write a 100-word Instagram post for Flexikitch targeting cafe owners. 
Include a hook, 3 benefits (cash flow, flexibility, maintenance), CTA to book consultation, and 5 hashtags.

## Intended Workflow or Task
Trigger: Marketing team schedules weekly social media contents
Integration: Output used directly in Instagram publishing tools like Meta Business Suite
Timing: Runs as part of content calendar like 3–5 posts per week
Next step: Interested users click CTA is redirected to lead capture form

Content planning → [P01 RUNS] → Post generated → Published → Lead engagement begins

## Problem Being Solved
Creating engaging social media content manually:
Time-consuming 
Inconsistent in quality and messaging
Often lacks clear conversion of focus

For a company like Flexikitch, poor content leads to:
Low engagement
Poor lead quality
Missed conversion opportunities

AI solves this by producing consistent, targeted, conversion-driven content 

## Automation Potential
Level: High
Dimension	             Assessment
Repetitiveness	       High — content creation is frequent
Data availability	     High — product + audience info known
Human judgment	       Medium — tone review required
Integration	Can connect to scheduling tools
Time saving	80 percent reduction per post

## Risks and Limitations
Risk	                              Level	      Mitigation
Generic or repetitive content	      Medium	    Add audience + constraints
Weak brand voice alignment	        Medium	    Human review before posting
Incorrect claims about services	    High	      Pre-approved benefit list
Over-reliance on AI	                Medium	    Mix AI + human creativity

Overall risk rating: MEDIUM 


# Prompt 1 - Version 1

Basic instruction with no context, Generic post with no clear target or CTA

## Observed Effect
Low engagement potential and not aligned with business goals
